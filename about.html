About

Ron Bronson designs information systems. For more than twenty years he’s worked across public institutions as a UX engineer, information architect, and service designer — making complex systems legible and building the structure that keeps them running.

He teaches service and interaction design at the University of Michigan’s Taubman College, and has founded and led civic design efforts including Portland Digital Corps, Design For The Public, and Portland Design Month. He speaks regularly on service design, public mechanics, and the consequences of design.

Outside the work, he coaches girls varsity tennis at Catlin Gabel School in Portland. His writing, talks, and reading live across this site — or you can reach him directly.
